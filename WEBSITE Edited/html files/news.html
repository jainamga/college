Micromax to invest Rs 200 crore to expand consumer electronics segments

NEW DELHI: Indian handset maker Micromax will invest Rs 200 crore into washing machines, refrigerators and air coolers, over the next 12 to 18 months, as it diversifies into new segments and moves towards becoming a consumer electronics brand.
The company expects consumer electronics to contribute 30%, up from 20% now, to the overall estimated revenue of Rs 5,400 crore by 2020, which will also be a 20% growth over its revenue estimate for the year ending March 2019.

Aqueous storage device needs only 20 seconds to go!

A KAIST research team developed a new hybrid energy storage device that can be charged in less than half a minute. It employs aqueous electrolytes instead of flammable organic solvents, so it is both environmentally friendly and safe. It also facilitates a boosting charge with high energy density, which makes it suitable for portable electronic devices.
